Consider two entwined paradigms.

1. Artistic medium

2. Communications media

There's a history here. It dates back to at least the early 1800s.
You could probably argue it's been forever.

Artistic medium

making | performing | expressing

"My medium is..."
"I work in the medium of..."

The medium is a substance.

Is the medium a means to an end? (an "instrument")
Or does it prescribe the end? ("lay bare the medium")

Communications media

storage (data) | transmission (signals) |
exchange (information)

"I recorded it on..."
"I shared it with..."
"I sent it by..."

The medium is a channel or container.

Recall the transmission model of mass communication.
Physical and semantic noise corrupt the fidelity of a message.

What's the relationship
between media + art
across the two paradigms?

Spoiler: we won't arrive at one conclusion.

Medium specificity

We'll be pretty reductive about each for the sake of the survey.
These approaches are really arguments about media + art.

exemplify each approach.

Media as Milieus

Foregrounds a world over medium specificity

Artists "despecify" media + tech

"a specific milieu of experience" (Rancière)

Check out Godard's Histoire(s) du cinéma.

How to intersect art + tech? Art + not art?

This approach is also called "media aesthetics" and even "relational aesthetics."
