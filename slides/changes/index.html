Three Tips
for Media Studies

1. Cite your source materials.

2. Select concrete, citable examples.

3. Identify + explain the "telling" details
of your example.

Quote things. Use screengrabs. Share URLs. Acknowledge all that stuff in your references.
Audiences like material they can consult on their own
(because critics are intermediaries).

Three More Tips for
Media Studies

4. Communicate the contradictions
(through one example whenever possible).

5. Use established terms (backed by research)
to save yourself time, reduce your word count,
and converse with folks in the field.

6. Avoid "media formalism." It evacuates
the content, context, and experience of media
(e.g., "TV is a passive medium,"
"Social media is interactive,"
"Video games make people...").

Save time (if you can) to revise and edit your work before circulating it.
Looking at it the next day = "fresh perspective."
