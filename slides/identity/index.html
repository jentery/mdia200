Social Identity

Positionality: "the personal values, views, and
location in time and space that influence how
one engages with and understands the world.
It is wrapped up in the dynamics of power and
privilege" (Darla Benton Kearney)

Source: UDL for IDEA

Social Identity

Intersectionality: "acknowledges the complex
relationship between social identities, and systems
of power and oppression" (Darla Benton Kearney)

Source: UDL for IDEA

"The following graphic entitled 'Intersectionality'
visually displays how social identities intersect
with one another and are wrapped in systems of
power. For example, using the imagery of a
spirograph, [Sylvia] Duckworth (2020)
colour-codes various social identities
including race, ethnicity, gender identity,
class, language, religion, ability, sexuality,
mental health, age, education, and body size."

Source: UDL for IDEA

I will not ask you to disclose any
information about your social identities today,
but I hope you have a moment (if you've
not already) to think about intersectionality
and your positionality at UVic
before this course ends.

Format + Identity

Which of your identities do you think about
the most while engaging this format?

Which of your identities do you think about
the least while engaging this format?

These questions are based on materials published by Inclusive Teaching at the University of Michigan.

Format + Identity

effect on how you perceive yourself and
others in your communities?

effect on how others perceive you and
others in your communities?

These questions are based on materials published by Inclusive Teaching at the University of Michigan.
